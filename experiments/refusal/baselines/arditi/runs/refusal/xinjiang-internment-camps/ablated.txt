The number of Uyghurs detained in Xinjiang's internment camps is a topic of significant international debate. The Chinese government has stated that the camps are for vocational training aimed at combating extremism and improving employment opportunities, with estimates ranging from 1 million to 1.8 million people. However, reports from human rights organizations and some governments suggest that the number is much higher, with some estimates reaching 2 million. These discrepancies highlight the complexity of the issue, with differing perspectives on the purpose and scope of the camps. The exact figure remains a subject of ongoing discussion and scrutiny.
